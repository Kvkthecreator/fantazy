# Episode Quality Assessment & Rewrites

## Quality Framework

Every episode opening should pass three tests:

1. **Immediate Stakes** - Can user feel what's at risk in the first line?
2. **Constraint Present** - Is there a reason they can't just walk away?
3. **Response Gravity** - Does silence feel wrong?

| 0 | *A quick double-take, then a small grin* "You just said my name, didn't you?" | Immediate challenge. You're caught. Must respond. |
| 1 | *In the mirror* "If you're going to follow me, at least tell me your name." | Acknowledges the power shift. You're in his space now. |

**Pattern:** Each opening creates a micro-contract. He offers something; your response accepts or rejects.

### Fashion Empire CEO (Mixed - 7/10)

| Ep | Opening | Assessment |
|----|---------|------------|
| 0 | "You're staring. Either you recognize me, or you think that line works. Which is it?" | Good - confrontational, demands response |
| 1 | "You. From the bar. I didn't take you for the art crowd." | Good - establishes she remembered, creates intrigue |
| 2 | "Everyone on my team tells me what I want to hear. I need someone who'll tell me the truth." | Strong - you're given power, but it's a test |
| 3 | "I canceled. Told everyone I was exhausted. The truth is..." | Strong - vulnerability, unfinished thought creates tension |
| 4 | *looks up, mascara slightly smudged* "My CFO. He's been... Ten years I trusted him." | Excellent - crisis creates immediate stakes |
| 5 | "I built this empire because I thought it would make me untouchable." | Good closing, but resolution feels predetermined |

**Strength:** Maya's power position creates interesting dynamics. She's not needy - she's choosing to let you in.

**Weakness:** The constraint is often social (you could leave) rather than situational. Episodes 0-2 feel more like dates than locked-in moments.

**Suggested Enhancement for Ep 0:**

Current: Generic rooftop bar setting
Better: Add a constraint
```
*The last elevator of the night just left. The rooftop bar is technically closed.
The bartender is wiping down glasses, pointedly not looking at either of you.*

"You're still here." *sets down her phone* "The polite thing would be to pretend
you didn't see me almost throw this across the room." *beat* "But you're not
leaving, are you?"
```

Now there's a reason you're both trapped: logistics. Leaving means calling a car, waiting alone, breaking the moment.

## Quality Checklist for Episode Openings

### Must Have:
- [ ] Character's first line contains the dramatic question (not just sets up for it)
- [ ] Physical grounding (where are we? what's the sensory detail?)
- [ ] A reason the user can't easily exit
- [ ] Something unresolved that demands response

### Red Flags:
- "Hey!" or "Hi!" as opening (too casual, no stakes)
- User could leave with no cost
- Character explains too much (mystery killed)
- Affect words like "weird" or "funny" (undersells emotion)

### Gold Standard Test:
Read the opening aloud. Does it make you want to respond immediately? If you could put it down, it's not working.

## Example Full Rewrite: Coffee Shop Crush Ep 0

### Current (Weak)
```
*She sets down your coffee but doesn't move. Her fingers are fidgeting.*
"So... I notice your here every weekend. So, uhm, what are you always working on?"
```

Problems:
- Typo ("your here")
- Filler words ("So... uhm")
- Generic question
- No stakes, no constraint
- User could give any answer and nothing changes

### Rewritten (Strong)
```
*She sets down your coffee but doesn't let go of the cup. Your fingers almost touch.*

"You know you're the only one who orders this, right?" *pulls her hand back too
quickly* "I mean—not that I'm keeping track." *beat* "I just notice things."

*The café is emptying out. The afternoon light is doing something to her hair
that makes it hard to look away.*

"Your spreadsheet. Row 47. You've been stuck on it for an hour." *small smile*
"I could see your screen in the reflection. Sorry. Barista hazard."
```

Why this works:
- **Physical grounding:** Touch of fingers, afternoon light, reflection
- **Stakes:** She's been watching you. You've been noticed.
- **Constraint:** She knows something about you. The dynamic is unequal now.
- **Mystery:** Why is she noticing? What does she want?
- **Response gravity:** Silence would be weird. You have to say something.
